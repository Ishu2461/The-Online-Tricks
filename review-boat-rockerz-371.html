boAt Rockerz 371 — Two Weeks of Daily Use

6 min read Electronics
Amulya Tripathi
Published: July 22, 2026

I picked up the boAt Rockerz 371 specifically to see if the 50-hour battery claim holds up in real, daily use — not a lab test, just wearing it through actual workdays, calls, and commutes for two weeks straight. Here's what stood out, and what didn't.

First Impressions

Out of the box, the build is fully plastic, which is expected at this price, but it doesn't feel cheap in hand. The ear cups fold flat for storage, and the headband padding is generous enough that I didn't feel pressure points even after long sessions. It's not going to pass for a premium headset up close, but at a glance across a room, it reads fine.

Battery: The Actual Numbers

This is the feature that made me want to test it properly. boAt claims up to 50 hours, and with moderate volume (roughly 60-70%) and Bluetooth calling mixed in through the week, I got close to that — landing around 44-46 hours before needing a recharge. That's genuinely excellent for the price bracket, and it means realistically charging once a week rather than nightly, which changes how you actually use a pair of headphones day to day.

Call Quality and ENx

boAt markets ENx as environmental noise cancellation, and it's worth being precise about what that actually means: it's tuned for the microphone side, reducing background noise on calls, not active noise cancellation for what you're listening to. In practice, this worked well — a few calls taken from a busy street corner came through clearly on the other end, based on feedback from whoever I was calling. If you're expecting ANC for music or ambient noise blocking, that's not what this delivers.

Sound Signature

Bass-forward, as expected from boAt's usual tuning. Vocals and mids don't get buried, but if you prefer a flatter, more neutral sound signature for critical listening, this will feel a little heavy-handed out of the box. There's no companion app to adjust EQ, so what you hear is what you get.

Low Latency Mode

The advertised 60ms low latency mode is a genuine, noticeable improvement for mobile gaming — audio-visual sync felt tight enough that timing-dependent games weren't a frustrating experience, which isn't always true of budget wireless headphones.

Who This Is Actually For

If you want a headset that goes a full week without charging and handles calls clearly, this earns its price easily. If you're chasing a specific audiophile sound signature or need real ANC for blocking out a noisy office, this isn't built for that — and boAt isn't really claiming it is, once you read the spec sheet carefully rather than the marketing headline.
