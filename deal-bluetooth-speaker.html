Why We're Featuring This

IPX7 waterproof rating at this price is genuinely uncommon — most budget speakers advertise 'splash resistant' rather than a real waterproof rating, and this one has the certification to back the claim, which is what made us dig into it further.

What You're Getting

A compact portable speaker with roughly 10-12 hours of playback, IPX7 waterproof rating (submersible, not just splash-proof), and Bluetooth 5.0 pairing. Decent bass output for its size, with a carry loop for clipping onto a bag.

Pros & Cons

What We Liked

  • Real IPX7 rating — actually usable near a pool or in the rain, not just marketing language
  • Battery life comfortably covers a full day out without needing a mid-day charge
  • Compact and light enough to genuinely carry around, not just 'portable' in name only

Worth Knowing

  • Max volume distorts slightly at the top end — great for personal or small-group listening, not built for large outdoor gatherings
  • No aux-in port on this model — Bluetooth only, so no wired fallback if your device's Bluetooth ever acts up

Our Take

For pool days, showers, or just knocking around outdoors without babying your speaker, the real waterproof rating here is the actual selling point worth paying for. If you mainly listen indoors and want maximum volume for a house party, you'd get more raw output from a larger, non-portable speaker at a similar price.
