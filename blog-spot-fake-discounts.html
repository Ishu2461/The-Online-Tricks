How to Spot a Fake Discount Before You Buy

5 min read Shopping Tips

"70% off" only means something if the original price was real. Inflated "was" prices are one of the most common tricks in online retail, and they're easy to spot once you know what to check.

Check Price History Tools

Browser extensions and sites that track historical pricing on major retailers let you see the actual price trend over months, not just the number shown today. If a product has genuinely sat at the 'discounted' price for weeks with an occasional price spike right before a 'sale,' that's your answer.

Be Wary of Round, Suspiciously High MRPs

A product with a listed MRP that no retailer has ever actually charged is a red flag — this is sometimes called a 'phantom MRP,' set artificially high purely to make the 'discount' look bigger.

Compare Across Multiple Retailers

If the same product is meaningfully cheaper elsewhere without any 'sale' framing at all, the discounted price you're looking at might just be closer to the real market rate — not a special deal.

Watch for Repeating 'Flash Sales'

If the same 'limited time' discount reappears every few weeks, it's not actually limited — it's just the regular promotional price, dressed up with urgency language.

Bottom Line

A genuine discount is verifiable against real price history, not just the number printed next to a strikethrough. Two minutes of checking before checkout saves you from the illusion of savings that isn't really there.
