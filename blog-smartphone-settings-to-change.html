5 Smartphone Settings You Should Change Today

4 min read Smartphones

Most phones ship with defaults tuned for the average user, not for your specific priorities. A few small setting changes noticeably improve battery life, privacy, and daily usability.

Turn Off Auto-Play Videos in Apps

Social media and news apps often auto-play video by default, which quietly burns both battery and mobile data. This is usually a one-toggle fix buried in each app's settings.

Review App Permissions Periodically

Apps accumulate permission creep over time — location access for apps that don't need it running in the background is both a privacy and battery concern. A quarterly permissions review is worth the ten minutes it takes.

Disable Non-Essential Push Notifications

Notification overload isn't just annoying — each one wakes your screen and pulls a small amount of battery. Trimming this down to what actually matters improves both focus and battery life.

Set Up Find My Device / Find My Phone Properly

This is the setting people skip until they actually need it. Confirm it's active and correctly linked to your account now, not after your phone is lost or stolen.

Bottom Line

None of these changes cost anything or require new hardware — just ten minutes in your settings menu for a meaningfully better daily experience on the phone you already have.
